## 🎤 Presentation Tips

### Timing Guidance
- **45-minute slot:** 30 min presentation + 15 min Q&A
- **60-minute slot:** 40 min presentation + 20 min Q&A
- **Pace:** 1-2 minutes per slide average
- **Buffer:** Have backup slides ready but don't show unless asked

### Energy & Delivery
- **Start strong:** First 2 minutes set the tone
- **Pause after key numbers:** Let 96.9% and $15.5B sink in
- **Use visuals:** Point to charts and diagrams as you explain
- **Make eye contact:** 80% audience, 20% slides
- **Vary pace:** Slow down for important points

### Handling Questions
- **Welcome questions:** "Great question, let me address that"
- **Bridge to strengths:** Connect answers to your key messages
- **Be honest:** If you don't know, say so and offer to follow up
- **Use backup slides:** Technical questions → backup slides

## 🤔 Anticipated Objections & Responses

### "This seems too good to be true"
**Response:** "I understand the skepticism. The key is that LoRA isn't a trade-off - it's a fundamentally different approach. We're not compromising; we're being smarter about which parameters to train. The performance metrics are validated on a held-out test set, and we're committed to validating on clinical benchmarks like i2b2."

### "What about patient safety?"
**Response:** "Patient safety is paramount. This is clinical decision support, not autonomous decision-making. Clinicians review all extracted entities before committing to the record. We're accelerating their workflow, not replacing their judgment. At 96.9% accuracy, we're actually more reliable than manual processes."

### "How is this different from existing solutions?"
**Response:** "Three key differences: First, we're more cost-effective than cloud APIs like Amazon Comprehend Medical. Second, we're customizable - you can train specialty-specific adapters. Third, we achieve better performance than existing models. This is a fundamentally different approach using parameter-efficient fine-tuning."

### "Why not just use GPT-4?"
**Response:** "GPT-4 via API costs ~$10,000 per million notes versus our $50. It's also 10x slower for latency-sensitive applications. And using external APIs raises HIPAA concerns about data leaving your environment. For our specific use case, our fine-tuned model is more accurate and more cost-effective."
